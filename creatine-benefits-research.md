# Creatine Research Summary

## What is Creatine?
Creatine is a naturally occurring compound found in muscle cells. It helps produce ATP (energy) during high-intensity exercise. Your body produces ~1g/day, and you get ~1g from diet (meat/fish).

## Proven Health Benefits

### 1. **Strength & Muscle (Most Researched)**
- Increases muscle phosphocreatine stores by ~20-40%
- Improves high-intensity exercise performance
- Increases muscle mass (when combined with resistance training)
- Typical gain: 5-15% strength increase
- Works for ~70-80% of people ("responders")

### 2. **Cognitive Function**
- May improve short-term memory
- May enhance brain performance under fatigue
- Some evidence for reducing mental fatigue
- Mechanism: Brain also uses creatine for energy

### 3. **Neurological Protection**
- Potential neuroprotective effects
- Being studied for: Parkinson's, Alzheimer's, Huntington's
- May reduce oxidative stress in brain cells
- Early research stage but promising

### 4. **Bone Health (Emerging)**
- May increase bone mineral density
- When combined with resistance training
- Mechanism: Supports osteoblast activity

### 5. **Blood Sugar Regulation**
- May improve glucose metabolism
- Could enhance glycogen storage
- May improve insulin sensitivity
- Evidence is moderate, needs more research

## Optimal Dosage

### Loading Phase (Optional)
- **20g/day** split into 4 doses (5g each)
- Duration: 5-7 days
- Purpose: Saturate muscles quickly

### Maintenance Phase
- **3-5g/day** (you're taking 5g - perfect)
- Timing: Doesn't matter much (post-workout slightly better)
- Duration: Continuous, daily

## Safety Profile

### ✅ Very Safe
- **Most researched sports supplement** (1,000+ studies)
- Safe for: Healthy adults, long-term use (up to 5 years studied)
- No kidney damage in healthy individuals
- No hormonal disruption

### ⚠️ Minor Side Effects
- **Weight gain:** 1-3 kg (water retention in muscles - normal)
- **Bloating:** Some experience stomach discomfort (take with food)
- **Cramping:** Rare, usually if dehydrated

### ❌ Contraindications
- Pre-existing kidney disease (consult doctor)
- Under 18 (not enough safety data)
- Pregnancy/breastfeeding (insufficient data)

## Interactions

### ✅ Good Combinations
- **Protein:** Synergistic for muscle growth
- **Carbohydrates:** May enhance absorption slightly
- **Caffeine:** Safe together (some debate about interference, but minimal)

### ⚠️ Watch Out For
- **Dehydration:** Drink extra water (you're already tracking this ✅)
- **NSAIDs:** Long-term high-dose NSAIDs + creatine may stress kidneys
- **Caffeine (timing):** Some suggest separating by 30 min (not critical)
